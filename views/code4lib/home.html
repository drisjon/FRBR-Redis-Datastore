Background

Using the Library of Congress Bibliographic Framework for the Digital Age as the starting point for software development requirements; the ${project.name} project is a proof-of-concept for a next-generation bibliographic NoSQL system within the context of improving upon the current MARC catalog and digital repository of a small academic library at a top-tier liberal arts college.

The ${project.name} project starts with a basic understanding of the MARC, MODS, and FRBR implemented using a NoSQL technology called Redis.

This presentation guides you through the theories and technologies behind one such proof-of-concept bibliographic framework for the 21st century.

Record-to-Cube

The underlying concept of the FRBR Redis Datastore is the Bibliographic Cube. Instead of trying to creating a single, flat canonical bibliographic record; the datastore orchestrates a network of key-value bibliographic associations with FRBR entities.

This is better visualized as moving from a flat record (Dublin Core, MARC21, MODS XML, etc.) to a cube of FRBR entities intersecting with parallel planes of metadata branes.

Engineering

Initially focusing on interoperability with existing MARC21 and MODS collections of records in legacy ILS and digital repository systems leveraging the existing work by the RDA Vocabularies and the Library of Congress. The ${project.name} uses SKOS representations of FRBR and RDA to map MARC21 and MODS records to the Redis datastore.

Other metadata schemes and future user-interfaces are in design stages for ${project.name}.
